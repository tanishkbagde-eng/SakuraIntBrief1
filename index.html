How to Use

— pro tips from the artists
01
Let Ink Dry First
When layering with watercolor, gouache, or Gelly Roll over Pigma, allow 60 seconds for the pigment to set. Dry pigment is fully water-resistant; wet ink isn't.
02
Vary Pressure for Line Weight
Light pressure gives a delicate hairline; firmer pressure thickens up. One pen does multiple line weights — great for expressive sketching demos.
03
Cap When Idle
Pigment ink dries fast — that's the feature, not the bug. Cap between strokes during slow demo work to keep the tip in peak shape across the full pack life.
